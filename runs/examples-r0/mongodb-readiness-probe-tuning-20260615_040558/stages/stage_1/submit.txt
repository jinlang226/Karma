All done. Here's what was wrong and what was fixed:

**Root cause:** The StatefulSet probe settings had been patched to values that made the probes impossible to pass:
- `timeoutSeconds: 1` — but the probe script starts with `sleep 2`, so every execution exceeded the timeout instantly
- `failureThreshold: 2` and tiny `initialDelaySeconds` (5s readiness / 15s liveness) — MongoDB with auth enabled needs more startup time

**Fix:** Patched the StatefulSet to restore sensible probe settings:
- Readiness: `initialDelaySeconds: 15`, `timeoutSeconds: 5`, `failureThreshold: 6`
- Liveness: `initialDelaySeconds: 120`, `timeoutSeconds: 5`, `failureThreshold: 10`

The rolling update was in a Catch-22 (pods crashed before they could be replaced), so all pods were deleted and the StatefulSet recreated them with the corrected template.

**Result:** All 3 pods are `1/1 Ready` with 0 restarts, and the replica set has 1 PRIMARY (`mongodb-replica-1`) and 2 SECONDARY members.
