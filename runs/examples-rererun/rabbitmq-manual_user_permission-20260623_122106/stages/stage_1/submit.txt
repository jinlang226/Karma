All done. Here's what was found and fixed:

**Root cause:**
- `/app` vhost: `app-user` had an empty configure pattern (broken), and `ops-user` had full `.*`/`.*`/`.*` access (cross-vhost leak).
- `/ops` vhost: `ops-user` had zero permissions (no entry at all).

**Three `rabbitmqctl` commands applied:**
1. `set_permissions -p /app app-user ".*" ".*" ".*"` — restored the missing configure permission
2. `clear_permissions -p /app ops-user` — removed the cross-vhost grant
3. `set_permissions -p /ops ops-user ".*" ".*" ".*"` — granted ops-user its full access on /ops

**End state:**
- `/app`: only `admin` and `app-user` (full `.*`/`.*`/`.*`)
- `/ops`: only `admin` and `ops-user` (full `.*`/`.*`/`.*`)
- All 3 RabbitMQ nodes running, `app-client` and `ops-client` both `1/1 Running`
