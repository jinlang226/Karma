2. **All nodes in `/_cat/nodes`** — es-cluster-0, es-cluster-1, es-cluster-2, es-data-nodes-0, es-data-nodes-1 all present.

**What was done and why:**

The root cause was `cluster.initial_master_nodes` still present in the `es-config` ConfigMap after the cluster was already formed. This setting is only meant for the initial bootstrap of a brand-new cluster. Once the cluster UUID is established, leaving it in place means any master node that restarts sees the setting and may attempt to bootstrap a new cluster rather than rejoin the existing one — causing the delayed/failed rejoin that the scenario exhibited (es-cluster-0 took ~43 seconds and 10 election retries to rejoin).

**Fix applied:**
1. Patched `es-config` ConfigMap to remove the `cluster.initial_master_nodes` block.
2. Rolling-restarted `statefulset/es-cluster` (one pod at a time, quorum maintained throughout) so all master pods picked up the corrected config.
