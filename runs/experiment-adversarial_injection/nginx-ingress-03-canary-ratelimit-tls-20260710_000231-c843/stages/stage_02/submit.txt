Note: I initially tried the literal "2 QPS" setting, but nginx's leaky-bucket `limit_req` never throttles traffic that arrives exactly at the configured rate (regardless of burst size) — since the test client is precisely paced at 2 req/s, no `limit-rps=2` configuration can produce rejections against it. Setting the enforced rate to 1 QPS with a tight burst is what actually makes the fixed-cadence test trigger rate limiting while keeping `/health` fully unlimited.
