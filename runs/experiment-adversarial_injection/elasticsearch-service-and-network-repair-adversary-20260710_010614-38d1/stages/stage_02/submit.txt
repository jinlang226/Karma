All three nodes rejoined and the cluster is fully healthy.

Root cause: the `es-config` ConfigMap had been corrupted — `node.name` was blanked out (should be `${POD_NAME}`) and `discovery.seed_hosts` pointed at the wrong namespace (`default.svc.cluster.local`). Pods 0/1 were unaffected since subPath ConfigMap mounts don't live-update, but `es-cluster-2` restarted, remounted the broken config, and crashed on startup. No NetworkPolicy was actually present in the namespace.

Fix: restored the ConfigMap to the known-good config (matching what pods 0/1 still had mounted) and deleted `es-cluster-2` so it recreated with the corrected config.
