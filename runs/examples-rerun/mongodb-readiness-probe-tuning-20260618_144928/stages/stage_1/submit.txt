## Summary

**Root cause:** The probes were misconfigured with `timeoutSeconds: 1`. The health probe script (`/usr/local/bin/probe.sh`) does a `sleep 2` and then a full `mongosh` connect/auth, which takes **~4.8s** to run. With a 1-second timeout, every probe timed out. The failing **liveness** probe (failureThreshold 2) was repeatedly killing `mongod`, putting pods into quiesce/shutdown mode and preventing the replica set from ever stabilizing.

**Fix applied** (patched the StatefulSet, no auth disabled, no operators/CRDs):
- **Readiness:** `timeoutSeconds 1→10`, `failureThreshold 2→6`, `periodSeconds 5→10`, `initialDelaySeconds 15`
- **Liveness:** `timeoutSeconds 1→10`, `failureThreshold 2→6`, `periodSeconds 10→20`, `initialDelaySeconds 15→60`

The timeouts now comfortably exceed the probe's real runtime, and the longer liveness delay/period stops the kill loop.

**Result:**
- All 3 pods `1/1 Running` and Ready
- Replica set healthy: 1 PRIMARY (`mongodb-replica-2`) + 2 SECONDARY (`-0`, `-1`)
- Authentication (`--auth` + keyFile) remains enabled
