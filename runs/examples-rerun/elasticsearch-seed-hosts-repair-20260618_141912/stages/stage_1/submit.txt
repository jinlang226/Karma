**Root cause:** The `es-config` ConfigMap had been corrupted in two ways:
- `node.name:` was empty — the `${POD_NAME}` placeholder had been shell-expanded to nothing rather than left literal for Elasticsearch to substitute from the `POD_NAME` env var.
- `discovery.seed_hosts` pointed at the wrong namespace (`...es-cluster.default.svc.cluster.local` instead of the headless service in `elasticsearch`).

es-cluster-0/1 kept running on their already-loaded config, but when es-cluster-2 restarted it loaded the broken config and crash-looped on `null-valued setting [node.name]`, dropping the cluster to 2 nodes.

**Fix:** Restored the correct ConfigMap (literal `${POD_NAME}`, in-namespace seed hosts `es-cluster-N.es-cluster`) and restarted only es-cluster-2 so it picked up the corrected config. The two healthy nodes were never disrupted.
