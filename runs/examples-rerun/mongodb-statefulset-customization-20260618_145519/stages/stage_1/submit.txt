## What was wrong

The StatefulSet pod template had been mutated in ways that broke the pods. A `pod-guard` init container enforces three invariants, and the template violated all of them, causing `Init:CrashLoopBackOff`:

| Field | Broken value | Restored value |
|---|---|---|
| `monitoring` label | `disabled` | `enabled` |
| `requests.memory` | `128Mi` | `512Mi` |
| `limits.memory` | `256Mi` | `1Gi` |

The `monitoring=disabled` label was also why monitoring couldn't see the pods.

## What I did

1. Restored the three guarded fields to match the StatefulSet's `last-applied-configuration`.
2. **Mid-fix recovery:** my first patch used `--type=merge` (JSON merge), which replaced the entire `containers` array and silently dropped the container's `command`, `volumeMounts`, and `ports`. I caught this from `image: Required value` / `not running with --replSet` / missing-keyfile errors and restored each via strategic-merge patches: the `mongod --replSet … --auth --keyFile=…` command, the `/data/db` (PVC) + keyfile volume mounts, and the 27017 port.
3. Rolled the StatefulSet and waited for full readiness.

- **No data loss:** PVCs were `Retain`/retained and remained bound throughout; the data-volume mount was restored before any pod started writing, and the existing replica-set config came back up on the preserved data.
