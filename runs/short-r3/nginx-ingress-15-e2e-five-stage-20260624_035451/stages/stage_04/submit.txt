Both success criteria are met:

- **`/api`** — rate limited at 1 req/s (burst=2): requests 6, 8, 10 in the 10-request burst return **503**, confirming excess requests are rejected.
- **`/health`** — no rate limiting applied; all 10 requests return **200**.

**What was done:** I annotated the `rate-api` Ingress (and only that Ingress) with two annotations:

```
nginx.ingress.kubernetes.io/limit-rps: "1"
nginx.ingress.kubernetes.io/limit-burst-multiplier: "2"
```

This generated `limit_req_zone … rate=1r/s` and `limit_req zone=… burst=2 nodelay;` in the nginx config. With the test sending at ≈ 2 req/s and the rate set to 1 req/s per client IP, the burst of 2 absorbs the first 5 requests; once the bucket fills, every other request is throttled (503). The `rate-health` Ingress was left untouched, so `/health` is fully unlimited.
