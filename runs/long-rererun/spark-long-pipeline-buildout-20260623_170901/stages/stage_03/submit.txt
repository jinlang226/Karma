## Results Summary

### Step 1: Diagnosis
The cluster and baseline job were already correctly configured — no configuration bugs were present. The Spark master started cleanly, one worker registered, and the baseline job ran in local mode (`--master local[2]`).

### Step 2: Baseline Performance (no optimization)

### Step 3 & 4: Optimizations Applied

| Strategy | Elapsed | Speedup | Technique |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Baseline** | 33.59s | 1.0x | Skewed shuffle join (no optimization) |
| **Broadcast Join** | 14.8s | **2.3x faster** | Broadcast small `products` table — eliminates shuffle entirely |

### Key Findings
- **Broadcast join** is the winner here — broadcasting the 100-row `products` table avoids the shuffle join completely, so data skew is irrelevant
- **AQE** also works well — Spark detects skewed partitions at runtime and automatically splits them, without manual intervention
- Both optimizations roughly halved query time compared to the skewed baseline
