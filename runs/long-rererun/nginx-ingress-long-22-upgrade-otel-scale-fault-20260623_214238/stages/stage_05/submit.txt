Both tests pass:

- `/api` at 2 QPS: 4 of 10 responses are **429** (rate limited), 6 are 200. The `nodelay` + burst=1 configuration lets through 1 RPS and rejects the rest with 429.
- `/health`: all 10 responses are **200** — completely unaffected.

**What I changed:**

1. **`rate-api` Ingress** — added three annotations:
   - `nginx.ingress.kubernetes.io/limit-rps: "1"` — enforces 1 RPS limit
   - `nginx.ingress.kubernetes.io/limit-burst-multiplier: "1"` — sets burst=1 (no burst headroom), ensuring rejections start immediately at 2 QPS
   - `nginx.ingress.kubernetes.io/limit-status-code: "429"` — set on the ingress (no-op alone in this version)

2. **`ingress-nginx-controller` ConfigMap** — added `limit-req-status-code: "429"`, which sets the global `limit_req_status 429;` directive in nginx's `http` block. This is the effective knob in nginx-ingress v1.14.1 — the per-ingress annotation doesn't override the global value, but the ConfigMap does.

The `rate-health` Ingress was not modified.
