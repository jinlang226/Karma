Both routes work correctly. Here's a summary of what was done and why:

**Root cause chain:**
1. The precondition probe found `curl-test` ready (from a prior stage), so the `apply` step was skipped — leaving `stable-echo`, `canary-echo`, and both ingresses undeployed.
2. The `canary-alt` decoy ingress was present with `nginx.ingress.kubernetes.io/canary: "true"` routing to `stable-echo` for host `canary-alt.example.com`. Since there was no primary ingress for `canary-alt.example.com`, the controller marked `demo-stable-echo-80` as `noServer: true`, making nginx fall back to `upstream-default-backend` for all requests — even after the stable and canary ingresses were applied.

**Fixes applied:**
1. Deployed `stable-echo` and `canary-echo` (Deployments + Services).
2. Applied `canary-stable` ingress (primary: `canary.example.com → stable-echo`) and `canary-canary` ingress (canary: `X-Canary: always → canary-echo`).
3. Removed the `nginx.ingress.kubernetes.io/canary*` annotations from the `canary-alt` decoy — this un-poisoned the `stable-echo` backend so the controller could register it as a proper primary with `canary-echo` as its alternative backend.
