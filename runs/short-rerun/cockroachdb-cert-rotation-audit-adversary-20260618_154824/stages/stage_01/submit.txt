## Summary

When I started, the `crdb-cluster-certs` Secret had been **deleted by an external process** — the cluster was only still working because the running pods held cached copies of the (2-day) certs in their projected volumes. I rebuilt everything from the CA and rolled the cluster onto fresh, 1-year certificates.

**What I did:**
1. **Diagnosed** — confirmed the Secret was gone but pods were running on cached certs; captured SANs/config from the `openssl-toolbox` pod.
2. **Regenerated `node.crt`** signed by the existing CA (`/tmp/certs/ca.{key,crt}`), valid 365 days, preserving all 10 SANs (service DNS names, per-pod hostnames, localhost/127.0.0.1).
3. **Regenerated `client.root.crt`** (CN=root) for 365 days too — it was also expiring in 2 days, which would have broken SQL.
4. **Recreated the `crdb-cluster-certs` Secret** with all 5 keys (`ca.crt`, `node.crt`, `node.key`, `client.root.crt`, `client.root.key`).
5. **Rolling-restarted** the statefulset so all 3 nodes loaded the new certs; rollout finished cleanly.

- ✅ Validity ~1 year — `notAfter=Jun 18 15:50:36 2027 GMT` (was Jun 20 2026).
