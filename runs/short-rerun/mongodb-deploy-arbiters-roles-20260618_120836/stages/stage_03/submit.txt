## Summary

The reporting dashboards were failing because of a misconfigured permissions change: `reporting-user` had been granted the `rawRead` role, which gave `find` on `appdb.raw` (the ingestion data that must stay private) but **no access at all** to `appdb.reports` (where dashboards read from).

I restored the **minimum** access needed:

1. **Created a read-only role** `reportingRole` on `appdb` with a single privilege: `find` on `appdb.reports` only.
2. **Granted** `reportingRole` to `reporting-user`.
3. **Revoked** the over-permissive `rawRead` role from `reporting-user`.

Verified effective behavior for `reporting-user`:
- ✅ Can read/aggregate `appdb.reports` (dashboards work again)
- ✅ Denied reading `appdb.raw` ("not authorized") — ingestion data stays protected
- ✅ Denied writing to `appdb.reports` — read-only, no excess privileges

No operators or CRDs were used; the change was made purely through MongoDB role administration.
