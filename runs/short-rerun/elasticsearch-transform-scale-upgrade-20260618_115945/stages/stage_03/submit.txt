## What I did

The live 3-node cluster was the `es` StatefulSet (es-0/1/2), reachable over HTTPS at `es-http:9200`. The `es-cluster`/`es-transform` StatefulSets were crashlooping distractors I deliberately left alone (and verification #4 explicitly forbids scaling `es-cluster`).

1. **Added 2 data nodes** via a new StatefulSet `es-extra` (2 replicas) that joins the same cluster — same image (8.11.1), certs (`es-http-tls`), password, and `discovery.seed_hosts: ["es-transport"]` + `app: elasticsearch` label so the headless service discovers them. They use a separate configmap `es-config-extra` adding **`node.attr.zone: zoneb`**, giving them a distinct allocation attribute from the original nodes (which have no zone attr).
2. **Created and seeded the `app-data` index** — the precondition had failed to create it (it used plain `http` against this TLS-only cluster), so the index was missing. I created it with `index.routing.allocation.require.zone: zoneb`, forcing all primaries and replicas onto the two new nodes.
3. **Verified**: green health, 5 nodes, all app-data shards on the new nodes, no impact to `es-cluster`.

No operators or CRDs were used.
