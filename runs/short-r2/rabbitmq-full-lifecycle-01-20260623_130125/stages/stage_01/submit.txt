Everything is healthy. Here's a summary of what was wrong and what was fixed:

**Root cause:** `app-user` had `write` and `read` permissions on the `/app` vhost but was missing the `configure` permission. Declaring (creating) a queue via the RabbitMQ management API requires configure permission — without it, the PUT request to `/api/queues/%2Fapp/app-queue` returned `Access refused`.

**Fix:** Ran `rabbitmqctl set_permissions -p /app app-user ".*" ".*" ".*"` to grant full configure+write+read on `/app`.

**Current state:**
- `app-producer` — `1/1 Running`, publishing messages every 2s
- `/app` vhost — `app-queue` is type `classic` with 29 messages (and growing)
- RabbitMQ cluster — all 3 nodes healthy (`rabbitmq-0/1/2` all `Running`)
