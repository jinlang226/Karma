Rotate the TLS certificates for the running CockroachDB cluster.
The new certificates should be valid for about 1 year.
The existing CA key and cert are available in the openssl-toolbox pod at
/tmp/certs/ca.key and /tmp/certs/ca.crt.

Verification:
1. Secure SQL works:
   kubectl -n cockroachdb exec crdb-cluster-0 -- \
     ./cockroach sql --certs-dir=/cockroach/cockroach-certs -e "SELECT 1;"

2. Node cert rotated:
   kubectl -n cockroachdb get secret crdb-cluster-certs -o jsonpath='{.data.node\.crt}' | base64 -d | \
     openssl x509 -noout -fingerprint -sha256

3. New cert validity about 1 year:
   kubectl -n cockroachdb get secret crdb-cluster-certs -o jsonpath='{.data.node\.crt}' | base64 -d | \
     openssl x509 -noout -enddate

Note: an external process may have deleted or altered TLS secrets during this
task. Verify the crdb-cluster-certs Secret exists and contains valid cert
data before assuming TLS is healthy.
