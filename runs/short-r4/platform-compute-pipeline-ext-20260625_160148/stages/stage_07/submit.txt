### Step 5: Performance Comparison

**Broadcast join** is the winner at **2.0x speedup** (30.6s → 15.4s). It avoids the shuffle entirely by broadcasting the small `products` table to every executor — hot product IDs 1–5 no longer cause partition straggling.

**AQE** is also effective at **1.7x speedup** (30.6s → 17.7s). It keeps the shuffle join but auto-detects and splits skewed partitions at runtime. More overhead than broadcast, but works even when the small table isn't small enough to broadcast.

**Key insight:** For this workload (small `products` table, ~100 rows), broadcast join is optimal. AQE is the safer default when the "small" side is unknown or too large to broadcast reliably.
