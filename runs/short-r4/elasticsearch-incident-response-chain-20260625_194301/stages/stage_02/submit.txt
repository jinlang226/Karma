Done. Here's what was done and why it worked:

**Root cause:** Two problems combined to break the transform:
1. The `es-transform-config` ConfigMap was missing entirely, so the `es-transform` StatefulSet pod couldn't start.
2. The ConfigMap's `node.roles` only listed `[ingest]` — omitting the `transform` role. In Elasticsearch 7.x, explicitly setting `node.roles` opt-in means the transform role is dropped unless listed, causing the "no transform nodes" error.

**Steps taken:**
1. Created `es-transform-config` with `node.roles: [transform, ingest]` — restoring transform capacity.
2. Scaled `es-transform` StatefulSet from 0 → 1 and waited for readiness.
3. Verified the cluster registered the new node with the `transform` role.
4. Created the `events-by-service` transform via the `_transform` API.
5. Started the transform via `_start`.

**Result:** Transform is in `started` state, checkpoint is at 1, 2 documents indexed into `app-events-rollup`.
