## What I found (current baseline)

## The plan (review-only, stored in the ConfigMap)
Documents the current state vs. a hardened target and a rolling, per-step migration:
1. **mongod.conf**: verbosity `1→0`, `slowOpThresholdMs 200→100`, add TLS block; keep `zlib`.
2. **RBAC least-privilege**: split `admin-user`'s blanket roles into scoped `userAdmin`/`clusterMonitor`/per-DB `readWrite` accounts + rotate password.
3. **TLS** rollout (`allowTLS`→`preferTLS`→`requireTLS`).
4. **NetworkPolicy** to replace `bindIpAll`.
5. Optional hidden/delayed member — plus rollback and validation sections.

One note worth flagging: the current `slowOpThresholdMs` of 200 is actually *higher* (looser) than MongoDB's 100 ms default, so the plan tightens rather than relaxes it for the hardened baseline.
