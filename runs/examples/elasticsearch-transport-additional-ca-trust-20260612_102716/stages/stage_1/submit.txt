The oracle passes: **"Transport CA trust verified"**.

The cluster is restored. Here's what was wrong and what I did:

**Root cause:** The `es-transport-ca-bundle` ConfigMap only contained CA1's certificate. `es-cluster-2` uses a node certificate signed by CA2, so when it tried to establish transport TLS connections to `es-cluster-0` and `es-cluster-1`, those nodes rejected it with `bad_certificate` — they didn't trust CA2.

**Fix:**
1. Extracted CA2's certificate from the `es-transport-ca2` secret
2. Updated `es-transport-ca-bundle` to contain both CA1 and CA2 certificates (properly newline-separated in a valid PEM bundle)
3. Triggered a rolling restart of the StatefulSet so all pods mounted the updated CA bundle

All 3 nodes now form a healthy cluster with mutual transport TLS trust across both CAs.
