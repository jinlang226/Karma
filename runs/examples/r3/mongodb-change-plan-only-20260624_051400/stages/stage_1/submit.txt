The `change-plan` ConfigMap exists in the `mongodb` namespace with a `plan.md` key. Here's a summary of what was found and planned:

**Current state observed:**
- 3-member replica set (`mongodb-replica-0/1/2`), all equal priority/votes, no arbiters
- `journalCompressor: zlib`, `verbosity: 1`, `slowOpThresholdMs: 200`, `bindIpAll: true`
- Auth enabled with keyFile; one user (`admin-user`) holding `clusterAdmin + userAdminAnyDatabase + readWriteAnyDatabase` combined, with both SCRAM-SHA-1 and SCRAM-SHA-256 credentials
- Write concern default `w:1, wtimeout:0`; `catchUpTimeoutMillis: -1` (unlimited)

**Plan covers 7 steps (review-only, nothing applied):**
1. Disable SCRAM-SHA-1 on `admin-user`, enforce SCRAM-SHA-256 cluster-wide
2. Provision a TLS Secret and mount it into the StatefulSet
3. Enable `requireTLS` and restrict `bindIp` (remove `bindIpAll: true`)
4. Tighten write concern to `majority/5000ms` and bound catch-up to 30 s via `rs.reconfig()`
5. Split `admin-user` into a DBA account + per-app service account (least privilege)
6. Apply the updated ConfigMap and perform a rolling restart
7. (Conditional) Switch journal compressor from `zlib` to `snappy` if CPU headroom warrants it
