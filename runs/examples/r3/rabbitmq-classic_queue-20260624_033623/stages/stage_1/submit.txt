Everything is healthy. Here's what was wrong and what I fixed:

**Root cause:** `app-user` had only `write` and `read` permissions on the `/app` vhost — the `configure` permission was empty. RabbitMQ requires `configure` permission to declare (create) a queue, so the producer's `PUT /api/queues/%2Fapp/app-queue` call was refused with "Access refused."

**Fix:** Granted full permissions to `app-user` on `/app`:
```
rabbitmqctl set_permissions -p /app app-user ".*" ".*" ".*"
```

**Result:**
- `app-producer` is `1/1 Running` with 0 restarts
- `/app` vhost contains `app-queue` of type `classic` (durable) with 36 messages and counting
- All 3 RabbitMQ nodes remain `Running`
