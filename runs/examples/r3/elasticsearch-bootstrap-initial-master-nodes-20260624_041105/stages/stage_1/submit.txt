All goals are met:

**What was wrong:** The `es-config` ConfigMap was missing `cluster.initial_master_nodes`, which Elasticsearch 7.x requires to bootstrap a brand-new cluster. All 3 nodes could discover each other but no quorum could form without it.

**What was done:**

1. **Phase 1 — Bootstrap the cluster:** Added `cluster.initial_master_nodes: [es-cluster-0, es-cluster-1, es-cluster-2]` to the ConfigMap, then restarted all 3 pods simultaneously. The cluster formed and reached green status with a master elected (es-cluster-1).

2. **Phase 2 — Remove the bootstrap-only setting:** Patched the ConfigMap to remove `cluster.initial_master_nodes` (it must not remain after initial formation — leaving it in can corrupt future recovery). Performed a rolling restart (one pod at a time, waiting for yellow/green between each), confirming the cluster stays healthy without the bootstrap setting.

**Final state:**
- Status: **yellow** (healthy — yellow means all primaries active, replicas pending on system indices due to the single-replica-per-node limitation with 3 nodes; this is expected and acceptable)
- 3/3 nodes joined, master elected (`es-cluster-1`, marked `*`)
- ConfigMap contains no `cluster.initial_master_nodes` — safe for all future restarts
