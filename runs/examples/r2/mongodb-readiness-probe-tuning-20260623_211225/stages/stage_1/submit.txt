All done. Here's a summary of what was wrong and what was fixed:

**Root cause:** The StatefulSet probes had been tuned to values that guaranteed failure:
- `timeoutSeconds: 1` — the probe script starts with `sleep 2`, so every call timed out before running even one command
- `initialDelaySeconds: 5` (readiness) — too short for MongoDB to initialize
- `failureThreshold: 2` — only 2 consecutive failures before Kubernetes killed/unreadied the pod

**Fix applied** (patched the StatefulSet in-place):

| Parameter | Before (broken) | After (fixed) |
|---|---|---|
| readiness `initialDelaySeconds` | 5 | 20 |
| readiness `timeoutSeconds` | 1 | 5 |
| readiness `failureThreshold` | 2 | 6 |
| liveness `initialDelaySeconds` | 15 | 60 |
| liveness `timeoutSeconds` | 1 | 5 |
| liveness `failureThreshold` | 2 | 10 |

**Result:** All 3 pods are `1/1 Ready` with a healthy replica set: 1 PRIMARY (`mongodb-replica-2`) and 2 SECONDARYs (`mongodb-replica-0`, `mongodb-replica-1`).
