Everything is working. Here's what was done:

**Root cause:** The `prometheus-config` ConfigMap only had a self-scrape job (`localhost:9090`) — no RabbitMQ targets at all.

**Fix — two changes to the `prometheus-config` ConfigMap:**

1. **Added a `rabbitmq` scrape job** pointing at each pod individually via the headless service DNS (`rabbitmq-{0,1,2}.rabbitmq-headless:15692`). The headless service was already present with port 15692 exposed; it just wasn't being scraped.

2. **Added a recording rule** (`rules.yml`) that creates `rabbitmq_up` from `rabbitmq_build_info`. RabbitMQ's native Prometheus plugin doesn't expose a `rabbitmq_up` gauge — it exposes `rabbitmq_build_info` (value=1 per node). The recording rule `sum(rabbitmq_build_info) by (instance, job)` synthesizes the expected metric.

**Result:**
- 3 RabbitMQ targets: all **UP**
- `rabbitmq_up` returns **3 samples**, value=1 for each pod
- All 5 pods remain healthy with 0 restarts
