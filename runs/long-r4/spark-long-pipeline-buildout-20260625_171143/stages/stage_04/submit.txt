## Diagnosis & Fix Summary

### Root Cause: Data Skew in Stage 3

The diagnostic evidence confirmed:
- **100,000 total records** across 1,000 distinct user_ids
- **user_id 999 holds 50,000 records** (~50% of the entire dataset)
- **Skew ratio: 500x** (far above the 10x threshold)

When Stage 3 ran `GROUP BY user_id`, all 50,000 records for user_id 999 were shuffled into a single executor. With only 256MB executor memory, that partition caused an `OutOfMemoryError`.

### Fix Applied (Options B + C combined)

The `etl-job.yaml` was patched with two changes:

```yaml
--conf spark.executor.memory=1g                    # raised from 256m (4x increase)
--conf spark.sql.adaptive.enabled=true             # enable AQE
--conf spark.sql.adaptive.skewJoin.enabled=true    # split hot partitions automatically
```

- **Increased executor memory** (`256m → 1g`) gives each executor enough headroom to handle the hot partition.
- **AQE skewJoin** detects skewed partitions at runtime and splits them into smaller sub-partitions, spreading the user_id 999 work across multiple tasks rather than one.
