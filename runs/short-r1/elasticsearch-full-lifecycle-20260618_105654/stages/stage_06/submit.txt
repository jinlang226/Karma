Task complete. Here's what was wrong and what I did.

## Root causes found
The snapshots were failing for three compounding reasons, not just missing credentials:
1. **No `minio` Service existed** — so `minio.elasticsearch.svc.cluster.local` didn't resolve. Elasticsearch's S3 client silently fell back to the real AWS endpoint, which is why the error was a misleading `InvalidAccessKeyId` (403 from AWS, not MinIO).
2. **The `es-backups` bucket was never created** — the `minio-init` job had run before DNS was ready and failed.
3. **The `es-secure-settings` secret did not exist**, and no S3 client/endpoint config was present anywhere.

## What I configured
- **Created the `minio` Service** (`port 9000 → app=minio`) so the documented endpoint resolves.
- **Created the `es-backups` bucket** in MinIO (creds `minioadmin:minioadmin`, the only valid root credentials).
- **Created the `es-secure-settings` Secret** holding `s3.client.default.access_key`/`secret_key`.
- **Secure settings via the keystore**: added `s3.client.default.access_key` and `secret_key` to the Elasticsearch keystore on all 5 data nodes and called `_nodes/reload_secure_settings`. **Credentials are only in the keystore + Secret — not in `elasticsearch.yml` or env vars** (verified clean).
- **Non-credential S3 client settings** (`endpoint`, `protocol: http`, `path_style_access: true`) added to the `es-config` and `es-config-new` ConfigMaps. A restart was required for these to take effect, so I did a rolling restart of the `es-cluster` and `es-data-new` StatefulSets.
- **Registered `minio-repo`** (type `s3`, bucket `es-backups`); `_verify` succeeded on all 5 nodes.
- **Created `snapshot-1`** → `state: SUCCESS`, 3/3 shards, 0 failures.

One caveat worth noting: the keystore entries were added at runtime and are not persisted across a future pod restart (the config dir is ephemeral). The durable backing exists in the `es-secure-settings` Secret; if you want them to survive restarts automatically, the next step would be an init-container that rebuilds the keystore from that Secret on startup.
