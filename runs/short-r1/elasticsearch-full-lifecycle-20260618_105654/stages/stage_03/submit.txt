## Summary

The newly added reporting user (`report-user`) couldn't authenticate because of a **file-realm merge gap**.

**Root cause:** The live Elasticsearch StatefulSet (`es-cluster`, ES 8.11.1) was not mounting the file-realm secret at all, and the new user lived in a *separate* secret that had never been merged into the realm the cluster reads:
- `es-file-realm-aggregate` (the secret the cluster is meant to mount) contained only `ops-user` (superuser).
- `user-provided-file-realm` held the new `report-user` (read), unmerged and unmounted.
- The `read` role assigned to `report-user` wasn't defined anywhere (ES 8 has no built-in `read` role).

**Fix (no operators/CRDs, native K8s only):**
1. **Merged** the user-provided entries into `es-file-realm-aggregate` — `users` and `users_roles` now carry both `ops-user` and `report-user`, preserving the existing user.
2. **Defined the `read` role** in the aggregate secret's `roles.yml` (read/monitor/view_index_metadata on indices), so the role mapping resolves.
3. **Patched the StatefulSet** to mount the aggregate secret as `config/users`, `config/users_roles`, and `config/roles.yml`, then rolled it.
4. Seeded the `app-data` index the reporting user reads.
