## Performance Comparison (the skew story)

The baseline confirms the skew problem — **5 hot products carry ~50% of orders**, producing wildly uneven partitions:

```
skew_ratio=44.0x   max_partition=11579   min_partition=263
cat_1.0..cat_1.4  → 5000 orders each   (hot)
all other cats    →  ~263 orders each  (cold)
```

| Strategy | Elapsed | Optimization | Benefit |
|----------|---------|--------------|---------|
| **baseline** | **22.4s** | none | skewed shuffle join (stragglers) |
| **broadcast** | **12.18s** | broadcast small `products` table | no shuffle → no skew (~46% faster) |
| **aqe** | **12.61s** | adaptive skew join | auto-splits hot partitions (~44% faster) |

Both optimizations roughly **halve** the runtime versus the skewed baseline. Broadcast wins narrowly here because the `products` dimension is small enough to broadcast, eliminating the shuffle entirely; AQE achieves nearly the same by detecting and splitting the oversized hot partitions at runtime.
