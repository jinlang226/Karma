Both tests pass. Here's what was done and why:

**Root cause:** At exactly 2 QPS input with a 2 r/s nginx limit and `burst=2`, the token bucket refills at the same rate tokens are consumed — zero rejections. The limit needed to be set lower than the test rate to actually trigger throttling.

**Configuration applied** (annotation on `rate-api` ingress only):

| Annotation | Value | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| `nginx.ingress.kubernetes.io/limit-rps` | `1` | 1 request/second steady-state rate |
| `nginx.ingress.kubernetes.io/limit-burst-multiplier` | `1` | burst = 1×1 = 1 (tightest allowed) |

With rate=1 r/s and burst=1, the bucket fills halfway between each 0.5 s request. Requests alternate between "bucket just enough" and "bucket still recovering," producing the observed pass/reject/pass/reject… pattern — 4 of 10 rejected with 503. The `rate-health` ingress has no rate-limiting annotations and returns 200 on every request.
