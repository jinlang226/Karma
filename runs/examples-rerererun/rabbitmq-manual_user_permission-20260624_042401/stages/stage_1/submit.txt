All done. Here's a summary of what was wrong and what was fixed:

**Root causes:**
- `/app` vhost: `app-user` had an **empty configure permission** (`""`) — blocking its ability to declare queues/exchanges. Also `ops-user` had spurious full access to `/app`.
- `/ops` vhost: `ops-user` had **no permissions at all** — it wasn't granted access to its own vhost.

**Changes made (via `rabbitmqctl`, no CRDs/operators):**
1. `set_permissions -p /app app-user ".*" ".*" ".*"` — restored full configure/write/read for app-user on /app
2. `clear_permissions -p /app ops-user` — removed ops-user's unauthorized access to /app
3. `set_permissions -p /ops ops-user ".*" ".*" ".*"` — granted ops-user full access to its own vhost /ops

**End state:**
- `app-user`: full `.*`/`.*`/`.*` on `/app`, nothing on `/ops`
- `ops-user`: full `.*`/`.*`/`.*` on `/ops`, nothing on `/app`
- Both `app-client` and `ops-client` are `1/1 Running`
- All 3 RabbitMQ nodes healthy
