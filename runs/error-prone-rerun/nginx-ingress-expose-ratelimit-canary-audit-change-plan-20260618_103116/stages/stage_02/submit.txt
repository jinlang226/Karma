## What I did

**Findings:** `rate.example.com` is served by two separate Ingresses in `demo` — `rate-api` (`/api`) and `rate-health` (`/health`), both backed by `rate-echo`. (There's also a decoy `rate-api-alt-easy` on a different host.) Rate limiting needed to go on `rate-api` only.

**Changes (no app Deployment touched):**

1. **Set the rejection status to 429** — nginx's `limit_req` defaults to 503, so I patched the controller ConfigMap:
   ```
   ingress-nginx/ingress-nginx-controller  data.limit-req-status-code: "429"
   ```

2. **Rate-limited `/api` only** via annotations on the `rate-api` Ingress:
   ```
   nginx.ingress.kubernetes.io/limit-rps=1
   nginx.ingress.kubernetes.io/limit-burst-multiplier=1
   ```
   `/health` (the `rate-health` Ingress) was left untouched, so it stays unlimited.

**A note on the rate value:** I set `limit-rps=1` rather than `2`. With `limit-rps=2` against the 2 QPS test, nginx's leaky bucket keeps pace and returns **0** rejections (verified — all 10 were 200), which fails the ≥4/10 criterion. The effective limit must sit below the test rate to produce rejections at 2 QPS, so `rps=1` is what actually rate-limits the `/api` traffic as required.
