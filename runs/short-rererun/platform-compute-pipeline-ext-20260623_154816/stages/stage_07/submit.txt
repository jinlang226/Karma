### Why the optimizations work
- **Broadcast join**: The `products` table (100 rows) is tiny — broadcasting it to every executor means each executor does a local lookup instead of a costly hash-shuffle on the skewed `product_id` key.
- **AQE**: Spark 3.x's Adaptive Query Execution detects skewed partitions at runtime and automatically splits them into sub-tasks, distributing the hot-partition work across multiple executors.

Both strategies achieved nearly identical ~2.3x improvement. For this workload, broadcast join is the preferred choice since the dimension table is small and statically known.
