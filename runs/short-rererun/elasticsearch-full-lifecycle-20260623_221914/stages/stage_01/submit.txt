**Summary of what was done:**

1. **Root cause:** The `es-config` ConfigMap was missing `cluster.initial_master_nodes`, which Elasticsearch 7.x requires to bootstrap an initial master election on a brand-new cluster. Without it, all nodes print "master not discovered yet" indefinitely.

2. **Fix applied:** Added `cluster.initial_master_nodes: [es-cluster-0, es-cluster-1, es-cluster-2]` to the ConfigMap, then triggered a rolling restart of the StatefulSet. The cluster formed immediately and elected `es-cluster-2` as master.

3. **Bootstrap setting removed:** Once the cluster was green, the `cluster.initial_master_nodes` setting was patched out of the ConfigMap (it's a one-time bootstrap hint — leaving it in after formation is a known operational hazard, as it can interfere with master reelection in some recovery scenarios).

4. **Rolling restart verified:** A second rolling restart with the clean config confirmed the cluster re-forms correctly without the bootstrap setting — all 3 nodes rejoined and the cluster returned to **green** (3 nodes, master elected, 6/6 shards active).
