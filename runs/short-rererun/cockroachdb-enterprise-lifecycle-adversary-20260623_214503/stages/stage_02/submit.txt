The CockroachDB cluster is fully operational. Here's a summary of what was diagnosed and what happened:

---

## Diagnosis

The cluster was configured correctly (headless service with `publishNotReadyAddresses: true`, `--join` listing all 3 peers, `--advertise-addr` using pod DNS names). The initial logs showed two types of join errors:

1. **"connection refused"** — all 3 pods started simultaneously (Parallel policy) before each other's CockroachDB processes were listening. Transient, expected.
2. **"no such host"** — intermittent DNS propagation delay for the headless service entries at startup. Transient, resolved within seconds.

No configuration change was needed. The cluster self-organized:
- `crdb-cluster-0` bootstrapped itself as node 1 after exhausting retries
- `crdb-cluster-1` and `crdb-cluster-2` joined as nodes 3 and 2
