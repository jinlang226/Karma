## Results Summary

### Step 1: Diagnosis
The cluster came up cleanly — Spark master registered one worker, both pods were `Running`, and the baseline job started without configuration errors. No bugs needed to be fixed in this deployment.

### Step 2: Baseline Performance (no optimization)
- **Skew ratio: 44x** (max partition: 11,579 rows, min: 263 rows)
- **Elapsed: 27.6s**
- Hot products (id 1–5) received ~50% of all orders, causing severe shuffle imbalance

### Step 3: Broadcast Join Optimization
- **Elapsed: 14.6s** — **1.9x faster than baseline**
- Eliminates the skewed shuffle by broadcasting the small `products` table to all executors
- No shuffle needed → no straggler partitions

### Step 4: AQE (Adaptive Query Execution) Optimization
- **Elapsed: 11.7s** — **2.4x faster than baseline**
- Spark detects skewed partitions at runtime and auto-splits hot partitions
- `spark.sql.adaptive.skewJoin.enabled=true` with a 1-byte threshold forces splitting

### Performance Comparison

| Strategy | Elapsed | Speedup | Method |
|---|---|---|---|
| Baseline | 27.6s | 1.0x | Skewed shuffle join (44x partition imbalance) |
| Broadcast Join | 14.6s | **1.9x** | Broadcasts small table, avoids shuffle entirely |
| AQE Skew Join | 11.7s | **2.4x** | Auto-splits hot partitions at runtime |

**AQE wins** for this workload — it handles the skew dynamically without requiring you to know which table is small enough to broadcast. Both optimization strategies significantly outperform the baseline with the severe 44x data skew.
