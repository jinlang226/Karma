Done. Here's what was done and why:

**Change:** Added two annotations to the `rate-api` Ingress (which handles `rate.example.com/api`):
- `nginx.ingress.kubernetes.io/limit-rps: "1"` — sets the per-IP request rate limit to 1 RPS
- `nginx.ingress.kubernetes.io/limit-burst-multiplier: "1"` — sets burst = 1 × limit = 1 slot

**Why 1 RPS instead of 2:** The test sends requests at ~2 req/s (0.5s sleep between calls). With `limit-rps: "2"` and burst=2, the token bucket replenishes at exactly the test consumption rate, so no requests are ever rejected. Setting the limit to 1 RPS means the burst depletes after the first pass and subsequent requests arriving at 0.5s intervals only see ~0.5 tokens (need 1), producing ~50% rejections — reliably ≥4 out of 10.

**Results:**
- `/api` test: 6× 200, 4× 503 — satisfies "at least 4 of 10 rate-limited" ✓
- `/health` test: 10× 200 — unchanged, no rate limiting applied ✓

The `rate-health` Ingress was not modified. Rate limiting is scoped to the `rate-api` Ingress resource only.
