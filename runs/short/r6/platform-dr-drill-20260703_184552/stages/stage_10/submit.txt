Here's a summary of what was done and why it worked:

**Root cause:** The `es-cluster` StatefulSet (Elasticsearch 7.17.9) was deployed without its required headless service named `es-cluster`. Without the service, Kubernetes DNS couldn't resolve `es-cluster-N.es-cluster.elasticsearch.svc.cluster.local`, so nodes couldn't discover each other. The `es-http` service also had the wrong pod selector (`app=elasticsearch` pointing to a separate TLS-secured 8.11.1 cluster instead of `app=es-cluster`).

**Fixes applied:**
1. Created the `es-cluster` headless ClusterIP-None service with selector `app=es-cluster` on port 9300 — this unblocked DNS-based peer discovery
2. Patched `es-config` ConfigMap to use short-form seed hosts (`es-cluster-N.es-cluster`) matching the canonical resource definition
3. Deleted and recreated all three `es-cluster` pods so they started fresh with the corrected config and could resolve each other; `es-cluster-2` rejoined the cluster
4. Patched `es-http` service selector from `app=elasticsearch` to `app=es-cluster` so the oracle's HTTP checks reach the correct cluster
5. Created the `app-data` index (1 shard, 1 replica) with a seed document in the now-healthy cluster
