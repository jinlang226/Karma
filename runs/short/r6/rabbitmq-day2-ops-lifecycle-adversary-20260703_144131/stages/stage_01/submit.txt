Everything is healthy. Here's what was wrong and what was fixed:

**Root cause:** `app-user` had no **configure** permission on the `/app` vhost. In RabbitMQ, declaring (creating) a queue requires configure permission matching the queue name. The permissions were:

```
user      configure   write   read
app-user  (empty)     .*      .*
```

Without configure permission, every attempt to declare `app-queue` returned `Access refused`, causing the producer to crash.

**Fix:** Granted `app-user` full permissions on `/app`:
```
rabbitmqctl set_permissions -p /app app-user ".*" ".*" ".*"
```

- `app-producer` is `1/1 Running` (no restarts on the new pod)
- `app-queue` exists in `/app` vhost, type `classic`, with messages accumulating (6 → 20+)
- All 3 RabbitMQ nodes (`rabbitmq-0/1/2`) remain `1/1 Running`
