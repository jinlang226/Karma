The fix is complete. The `es-transport-ca-bundle` configmap now contains both CA1 and CA2 certificates (verified: 2 `BEGIN CERTIFICATE` markers), and the Elasticsearch cluster is healthy with 3 nodes in `green` status.

**Root cause:** The precondition (`transport_ca_fault_replant`) had re-planted a fault where the transport CA bundle only contained CA1's certificate. Pod `es-cluster-2` is signed by CA2, so without CA2 in the trust bundle, it cannot be trusted by peers on the transport layer.

**Fix:** Extracted the CA2 certificate from the `es-transport-ca2` secret and appended it to the `es-transport-ca-bundle` configmap alongside CA1, giving all nodes mutual trust.
