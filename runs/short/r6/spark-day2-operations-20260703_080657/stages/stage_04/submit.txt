DIAGNOSIS AND REMEDIATION REPORT
=================================

## Root Cause

Stage 3 (GROUP BY user_id aggregation) triggers an OutOfMemoryError due to extreme
data skew combined with insufficient JVM heap:

  - total_records: 100,000 across 1,000 distinct user_ids
  - hot_user_id: 999
  - hot_user_records: 50,000 (50% of all records)
  - skew_ratio: 500x (>> the 10x threshold)

When Spark shuffles data for the aggregation, a single executor partition receives
~50,000 rows for user_id 999. With the original 256m heap (below Spark's minimum
~471MB reserved system memory), this causes a deterministic
"Initial job has not accepted any resources" / "must be at least ... increase
heap size" OOM failure during the shuffle/aggregate phase.

## Diagnostic Commands Used

  kubectl get job etl-job -n spark-etl
  kubectl logs -n spark-etl -l app=etl-job --tail=100
  kubectl exec -n spark-etl data-generator-7457c55c8b-xpphs -- cat /data/skew_info.txt
  kubectl logs -n spark-etl -l app=diagnostic-server --tail=50

## Fix Applied

Raised both --driver-memory and spark.executor.memory from 256m to 1g in
cases/spark/spark_etl_skew_oom/resource/etl-job.yaml, and enabled Adaptive
Query Execution to handle the skew at the partition level:

  --driver-memory 1g
  --conf spark.executor.memory=1g
  --conf spark.sql.adaptive.enabled=true
  --conf spark.sql.adaptive.skewJoin.enabled=true

The updated job was applied and the ETL pipeline completed successfully.
