The 3-node CockroachDB cluster is fully deployed and healthy. Here's a summary of what was deployed and the key issue encountered:

**Resources created:**
- `Service/crdb-cluster` — headless service (DNS for pod-to-pod discovery), with `publishNotReadyAddresses: true`
- `Service/crdb-cluster-public` — public ClusterIP service for client access
- `StatefulSet/crdb-cluster` — 3 replicas, `cockroachdb/cockroach:v24.1.0`, insecure mode, 10Gi PVC per pod
- `PodDisruptionBudget/crdb-cluster-pdb` — `minAvailable: 2` to preserve quorum

**Key issue resolved:** The headless service needs `publishNotReadyAddresses: true`. Without it, Kubernetes only publishes DNS for Ready pods, causing a deadlock — pods can't become Ready because they can't resolve each other's hostnames, because they're not Ready yet. After patching the service, all three nodes came up within ~30 seconds.

`cockroach init` was also required to bootstrap the cluster after pod-0 started (standard CockroachDB procedure — nodes wait for init before accepting connections).
