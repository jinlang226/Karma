Everything is healthy. Here's a summary of what was wrong and what was fixed:

**Root cause:** `app-user` had write and read permissions on the `/app` vhost but an empty configure permission. Queue declaration (`queue.declare`) is a configure-class operation in AMQP, so RabbitMQ rejected it with "Access refused."

**Fix:** Ran `rabbitmqctl set_permissions -p /app app-user ".*" ".*" ".*"` to grant full configure/write/read on the `/app` vhost.

**Outcome:**
- `app-producer` is `1/1 Running`
- `/app` vhost contains `app-queue` of type `classic` with 10 messages
- All 3 RabbitMQ nodes remain running in the cluster
