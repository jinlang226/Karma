## What was there

Four Ingresses share `host: canary.example.com` concerns:

| Ingress | Role | Backend | Canary annotations |
|---|---|---|---|
| `canary-stable` | main (no canary annotation) | `stable-echo` | — |
| `canary-canary` | canary | `canary-echo` | `canary: true`, `canary-by-header: X-Canary`, `canary-by-header-value: always` |
| `canary-alt` | decoy (different host `canary-alt.example.com`) | `stable-echo` | value `beta` |

The `canary-canary` Ingress is correctly configured: NGINX's `canary-by-header: X-Canary` + `canary-by-header-value: always` means a request carrying `X-Canary: always` is routed to the canary backend (`canary-echo`), while requests without that header fall through to the main `canary-stable` Ingress → `stable-echo`.

The controller access logs confirm the split routing — requests resolve to `[demo-stable-echo-80]` normally and to the alternative backend `[demo-canary-echo-80]` when the header is present.
