All requirements are met. Here's a summary of what was done:

**Root cause:** The `prometheus-config` ConfigMap only had a scrape job for `localhost:9090` (Prometheus self-monitoring) — no RabbitMQ scraping was configured.

**Fix applied** (two patches to `prometheus-config`):

1. **Added a `rabbitmq` scrape job** targeting all 3 pods via their stable headless service DNS names (`rabbitmq-{0,1,2}.rabbitmq-headless:15692`) with `metrics_path: /metrics`.

2. **Added a recording rule** that maps `up{job="rabbitmq"}` → `rabbitmq_up`, since the native RabbitMQ Prometheus plugin (port 15692) exposes many `rabbitmq_*` metrics but not `rabbitmq_up` itself — that metric is only produced by the third-party `rabbitmq_exporter`.

**Result:**
- 3 RabbitMQ targets are `UP` in Prometheus
- `rabbitmq_up` returns 3 samples, all with value `1`
- All 3 RabbitMQ pods remain healthy (`1/1 Running`)
