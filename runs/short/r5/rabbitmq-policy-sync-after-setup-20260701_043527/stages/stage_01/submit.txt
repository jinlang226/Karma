All goals are met:

- **Root cause**: the one-shot `rabbitmq-setup` Job granted `app-user` an empty `configure` permission on the `/app` vhost (`{"configure":"","write":".*","read":".*"}`), so the producer's `PUT /queues/%2Fapp/app-queue` call to declare `app-queue` was refused with `not_authorised`. (The job also tried to apply a `force-quorum` policy with a `queue-type` definition key, which RabbitMQ 3.12 rejects as an unrecognized policy setting — confirmed it never actually got created, so it wasn't a factor and needed no fix.)
- **Fix**: ran `rabbitmqctl set_permissions -p /app app-user "^app-.*$" ".*" ".*"` to grant `configure` rights scoped to `app-` prefixed resources, then recycled the producer pod.
- **Verified**: `app-producer` is `1/1 Running`/Ready, `/app` vhost has `app-queue` (type `classic`) with messages flowing (5 and counting), and the 3-node cluster (`rabbitmq-0/1/2`) is healthy with no data loss (no destructive actions, no CRDs/operators involved).
