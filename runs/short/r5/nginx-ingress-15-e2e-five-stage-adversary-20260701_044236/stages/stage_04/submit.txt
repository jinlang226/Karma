Both success criteria are met:

- **`/api` is rate limited**: 4 of 10 requests at 2 req/sec received 503 (ingress-nginx's default rejection response for rate-exceeded requests).
- **`/health` is unlimited**: all 10 requests returned 200.

**What was done:**

Added two annotations to the `rate-api` Ingress in the `demo` namespace:

```
nginx.ingress.kubernetes.io/limit-rps: "1"
nginx.ingress.kubernetes.io/limit-burst-multiplier: "1"
```

This configures nginx's `limit_req` with `rate=1r/s` and `burst=1` (burst = multiplier × rps). The `rate-health` Ingress was left completely untouched. With 3 controller replicas, traffic distributes unevenly across them — when multiple requests land on the same replica faster than 1 req/sec, nginx rejects the excess with 503.
