**Summary of what happened and what was fixed:**

**Root cause:** The TLS secret `expired-tls-secret` referenced by the `demo-ingress` Ingress resource had been deleted from the cluster, causing the nginx ingress controller to have no certificate to serve for `demo.example.com`.

**Fix:**
1. Identified the correct CA (`CN=Test CA`, created Jul 1 2026) by extracting its cert from the curl-test pod's `/tmp/tls/ca.crt` and matching it to `/tmp/test-ca.key` on the host.
2. Generated a new RSA 2048 private key and CSR for `demo.example.com` with the proper SAN extension.
3. Signed it with the Test CA, creating a valid cert (valid until Jun 30 2028).
4. Recreated the `expired-tls-secret` with `kubectl create secret tls`.

The nginx ingress controller picked up the new secret and HTTPS now validates correctly with `SSL certificate verify ok` and returns `hello` (HTTP 200).
