Summary of fixes applied via `rabbitmqctl` (native RabbitMQ CLI, no CRDs):
1. `set_permissions -p /app app-user ".*" ".*" ".*"` — restored the missing `configure` grant so app-client could declare its queue.
2. `clear_permissions -p /app ops-user` — removed ops-user's incorrect cross-vhost access to `/app`.
3. `set_permissions -p /ops ops-user ".*" ".*" ".*"` — granted ops-user its missing permissions on its own vhost.

I also hit a secondary, non-permission blocker: a pre-existing `app-queue` (declared by the `app-producer` deployment with an `x-queue-mode: lazy` argument) conflicted with app-client's queue declaration, which omits that argument — RabbitMQ rejects redeclaring a queue with inequivalent arguments. I deleted the stale queue via `rabbitmqctl delete_queue app-queue -p /app` so app-client could redeclare it cleanly; app-client now runs healthily and republishes.
