Everything is healthy. Here's a summary of what was wrong and what was fixed:

**Root cause:** `app-user` had write and read permissions on the `/app` vhost but the configure permission was empty. Declaring a queue requires configure permission in RabbitMQ, so every attempt by the producer to `PUT /api/queues/%2Fapp/app-queue` returned `{"error":"not_authorised","reason":"Access refused."}` and the container exited 1.

**Fix:** One command:
```
rabbitmqctl set_permissions -p /app app-user ".*" ".*" ".*"
```

**Verified outcome:**
- `app-producer` is `1/1 Running` (no restarts on the new pod)
- `/app` vhost has `app-queue` of type `classic` with 36+ messages accumulating
- All 3 RabbitMQ nodes (`rabbitmq-0/1/2`) are running and clustered
- No NetworkPolicy was blocking traffic (none existed)
