## Bug Fixed

**`skew-job-aqe.yaml` — invalid AQE threshold**

The embedded Python script (base64-encoded in the YAML) had:
```python
spark.conf.set("spark.sql.adaptive.skewJoin.skewedPartitionThresholdInBytes", "1b")
```
Setting the threshold to **1 byte** makes every partition look skewed, causing AQE to misfire on all partitions rather than selectively splitting the hot ones. Fixed to `"256mb"` — the standard sensible default.

## Performance Comparison

| Strategy | Elapsed | Optimization | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|---|
| **baseline** | 17.6s | none | Skewed shuffle join — straggler partitions block completion |
| **broadcast** | 8.59s | broadcast join | Products table (<100 rows) replicated to each executor; no shuffle, no skew |
| **aqe** | 9.09s | AQE skew join | Spark auto-detects and splits hot partitions at runtime |

**Data skew confirmed:** 44x ratio (max partition 11,579 rows vs min 263) from the 5 hot products (product_id 1–5) receiving 50% of all orders.

Both optimizations deliver ~2x speedup over baseline:
- **Broadcast join** eliminates the shuffle entirely — best when one side fits in memory.
- **AQE** works transparently at runtime — best for cases where you can't predict skew ahead of time or the smaller table is too large to broadcast.
