All pods are healthy. Here's a summary of what was fixed:

**Root cause:** Two permission gaps in RabbitMQ:
1. `app-user` had no permissions on `/app` — only `admin` and (incorrectly) `ops-user` did.
2. `ops-user` had no permissions on `/ops` — only `admin` did.

**Fixes applied:**
1. `rabbitmqctl set_permissions -p /app app-user ".*" ".*" ".*"` — gave `app-user` full configure/write/read on `/app`.
2. `rabbitmqctl set_permissions -p /ops ops-user ".*" ".*" ".*"` — gave `ops-user` full configure/write/read on `/ops`.
3. Deleted the stale `app-queue` that had an `x-queue-mode=lazy` property mismatch (it had been left over from a previous declaration), allowing `app-client` to redeclare it cleanly.

All 3 RabbitMQ nodes are running, both `app-client` and `ops-client` are `1/1 Running`.
