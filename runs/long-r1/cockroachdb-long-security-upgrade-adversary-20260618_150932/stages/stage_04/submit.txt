TLS certificate rotation complete and verified. Summary:

## What I found
- The running CockroachDB cluster (`crdb-cluster` StatefulSet, 3 nodes, v24.1.0) was mounting secret **`crdb-certs`**, which was signed by a **rogue CA** (`O=Cockroach, CN=Cockroach CA`) — *not* the legitimate CA in the openssl-toolbox pod.
- The legitimate secret **`crdb-cluster-certs`** was signed by the real CA (`CN=CockroachDB CA`, matching `/tmp/certs/ca.crt`) but its node cert was **expiring in ~2 days** (Jun 20 2026).

## What I did
1. Generated a fresh **node cert** and **client root cert** signed by the legitimate toolbox CA (`/tmp/certs/ca.{key,crt}`) with `--lifetime=8760h` (~1 year), replicating the full SAN set.
2. Updated the **`crdb-cluster-certs`** secret with the new node/client/ca material.
3. Repointed the StatefulSet from the rogue `crdb-certs` back to the legitimate `crdb-cluster-certs`.
4. Restarted all 3 pods together (avoiding a mixed-CA gossip window); cluster reformed cleanly from persistent storage.

## Verification (all pass)
1. **Secure SQL works** — `SELECT 1;` returns `1`; all 3 nodes report healthy.
